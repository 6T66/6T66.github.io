Historical Context

Historical Accuracy and Context

Now, to discuss the historical relavence and accuracy/context. When it comes to the overall story, it was
very realistically represented. At the beginning of the story, Jonas is confused as to why they are being deported.
He soon learned why and since then was toughened and changed as many were due to mass deportation from their
homes with no apparent reason why. This demonstrates the numerous hardships that the people from the Baltic
states. The starvation that Stalin inflicted on the population was horrible yet shown very accurately in the
story in the case that food was limited to the general population. Through the use of violence and cruelty the
NKVD portray the treatment that the real people most likely felt as they were oppressed for committing no real
crimes in the case of trying to go somewhere else or helping someone such as Kostas Vilkas, Lina's father in the
book, "Between Shades of Gray." Overall, the book does a very good job of portraying the events that happened whether
someone being starved or beaten or mistreated in any way by the police and military of Russia. I would say, if
it was shorter would be a very good source of first-person information.

Character Page

Character Page

This is a character profile on Lina Arvydas. Lina was described as a slender frame much like her mother Elena.
She was apparently beautiful as she fell in love with a described person such as Andrius and apparently married
him later on. Her emotions on the other hand were often conflicted within the story. With having to leave Andrius
towards the end, furthermore, she constantly had to think about "Breaking up with him" as their relationship
might have caused problems later on in the survelliance camps that they were put in after the ordeals in the
regions of Siberia. Their were many things that Lina did throughout the story that affected the other characters.
Things including the emotional support that she gave to her mother, Jonas, and the rest of the group. These
actions kept a large majority of them going. Her inner thoughts were always considerate of others in the fact
that, unlike the bald man, wanted to see everyone survive the cold and go home to Lithuania. The combination
of all of these things creates the presence of Lina being a good character in the sense that her intentions
were always to the best of everyone else first compared to herself.
